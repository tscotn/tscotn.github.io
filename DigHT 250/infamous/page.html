Jeff Probst Bot

This is a bot that scrambles a set of roughly 20 using a series of if statements and random number generators...it's pretty rough, and prone to repetition. Jeff Bot's been running for almost a year, and has only accrued about 9 followers, mostly friends who were present when I was working on the project written in Python. It tweets once every afternoon.

Nobody's Talking About

The phrase "nobody's talking about" seems to pop up on Twitter every few days, usually concerning a topic that a great number of people are talking about. To track trends and see if people truly aren't talking about something, I built a bot that retweets tweets with the phrase "Nobody's talking about" every two hours. It's gained 18 followers almost entirely organically over a handful of months.

Tweets by HowNobody

There Should Be An App

I built this bot the same day as "Nobody's Talking About" with the same idea--to track instances of people tweeting out their app ideas. Over a couple of months, I've found that a surprising number of people have had the idea to build an app to track ice cream trucks and that a lot of people don't realize their app idea already exists. So far, this is my most popular but with 29 organic followers, which seems to be growing exponentially.

Ed Yong Bot

Most of the more problematic bots just post political spam or tweet out disinformation. This bot was an effort in exploring what the process of building a bot might look like, as well as an experiment with Markov Chains. This bot generates tweets based off of a corpus of articles by the journalist Ed Yong at The Atlantic. Sometimes it produces gibberish, sometimes it's hilarious, and sometimes it's uncannily realistic. So far, my wife and I are the only followers.
